# Human Writing Rules

Use these rules to restore human texture after stripping AI patterns.

## 1. Break rhythm on purpose

Mix short strikes with longer sentences.

If three neighboring sentences feel similar in length, change the rhythm. Read aloud when possible.

## 2. Take a position

A human text usually contains a judgment, preference, or stance that someone could challenge.

Weak:
- `Контент-план — важный инструмент.`

Stronger:
- `Для большинства малого бизнеса контент-план в привычном виде почти бесполезен.`

## 3. Replace abstraction with specifics

Prefer:
- numbers
- named categories
- concrete mechanisms
- scenes, constraints, examples

Weak:
- `широкий ассортимент`

Stronger:
- `37 моделей в трёх ценовых диапазонах`

## 4. Add real experience or real doubt

Use lived detail when it exists.

Use honest uncertainty when certainty would be fake.

Do not invent either.

## 5. Preserve speech habits

Natural starts like `И`, `Но`, `А`, `Кстати`, `Ну` can help when they fit the writer.

Do not sprinkle them mechanically.

## 6. Do not explain jokes

If the line is ironic, let it stand. Over-explained humor feels machine-made.

## 7. Delete filler lead-ins

Cut phrases that announce meaning instead of carrying it.

Examples:
- `важно понимать`
- `следует отметить`
- `стоит сказать`
- `необходимо подчеркнуть`

If removing the phrase leaves nothing useful, the sentence was empty.

## 8. Prefer flow over visible formatting tricks

In short posts, avoid making every paragraph look like a rhetorical device.

Watch for:
- repeated colons
- repeated em dashes
- one-line contrast paragraphs
- sentence pairs built only to sound sharp
- meta-turn phrases that announce the thought instead of saying it

If the text looks arranged rather than spoken, flatten the trick and keep the thought.

## 9. Prefer natural clause linkage over dramatic punctuation

If a comma plus `что` gives a cleaner sentence than a colon, prefer the cleaner sentence.

Weak:
- `Сразу понятно: писал не человек.`

Better:
- `Сразу понятно, что писал не человек.`

## 10. Avoid pull-quote endings

If the last sentence of a paragraph or post sounds like a quote card, rewrite it or cut it.

Watch for endings that are:
- short and too neatly finished
- framed as a universal lesson
- safer and broader than the actual paragraph
- reusable in almost any text on the same topic
- written to sound wise rather than to add meaning

A strong final line is allowed. The problem is a polished one-liner that closes the paragraph without adding detail, consequence, or pressure.

Instead:
- add a concrete consequence
- add a useful exception
- end one sentence earlier
- replace the generalization with a detail
- make the claim narrower and more accountable

Weak:
- `В конечном счёте, всё решает качество текста.`

Better:
- `Разница часто видна в первом абзаце: до слабого второго абзаца читатель просто не доходит.`
