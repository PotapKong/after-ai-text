# Misha Anti-Patterns

This file captures user-specific signals of “neural smell” in Russian writing.

Use it as a stricter layer on top of the generic anti-AI rules.

## Avoid these patterns

### 1. Symmetrical contrast frames

These often feel pre-built rather than lived.

Examples to avoid:
- `не на X, а на Y`
- `не в X дело`
- `дело не в X`
- `не ради X, а ради Y`
- `не ради X и не ради Y`
- `не про X, а про Y`
- quoted comparison pairs such as `не как "X", а "Y"`
- any sentence that stages identity through two quoted options

### 2. Turn-announcing wrappers

These announce a shift instead of carrying it naturally.

Examples to avoid:
- `проблема в другом`
- `вот в чем подвох`
- `и вот почему`
- `а текст начинается позже`

### 3. Prettified pseudo-insight phrases

These can sound polished but artificial.

Examples to avoid or treat with suspicion:
- `температура текста`
- `текст начинает дышать`
- `за текстом кто-то стоит`
- `внутреннее напряжение автора`
- `под другим углом`
- `неприятная ловушка`
- `под ногами стало твёрже`

### 4. Repeated rhetorical punctuation

- repeated colons in short prose
- em dashes where a comma or ordinary clause sounds more natural

Prefer normal syntax when it reads cleaner.

### 5. Over-neat punch fragmentation

Short fragments can work, but watch for over-designed lines such as:
- `Свой. Спокойный. Без суеты.`
- any serial one-line impact rhythm that feels composed for effect

## Preferred direction

- plain but not flat
- human rhythm without visible devices
- direct thought without wrappers
- less performance, more observation
- calm tone over artificial sharpness

## Editing rule

If a sentence sounds “well-written” before it sounds true, simplify it.
