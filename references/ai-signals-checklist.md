# AI Signals Checklist

Use this file to diagnose whether a draft reads like generic AI output.

## Core signals

1. **Perfect grammar with no friction**
   - No typos, no restarts, no natural roughness.

2. **Monotone sentence rhythm**
   - Most sentences are close in length.
   - The draft feels metrically even.

3. **Emotionally safe tone**
   - Avoids sharp judgments, tension, sarcasm, or risk.

4. **Template structure**
   - Predictable intro → body → conclusion with no real movement.

5. **No visible doubt**
   - Sounds certain everywhere, or uses soft hedging everywhere.

6. **No personal prism**
   - No lived detail, no situated observation, no concrete viewpoint.

7. **Repeated constructions**
   - Same phrase shape appears many times without intent.

8. **Dash overuse**
   - Overuses em dashes where commas or a simpler structure would work.

9. **Over-neat typography**
   - Quote style or punctuation feels mechanically correct rather than natural for the context.

10. **No real humor or edge**
   - Jokes explain themselves or never risk misunderstanding.

11. **No speech habits**
   - No trace of how a real person usually starts, turns, or lands a sentence.

12. **Sliding logic**
   - Paragraph transitions are formally smooth but mentally empty.

13. **Imported formatting habits**
   - English-first punctuation or list conventions leak into Russian writing.

14. **Safe agreement with everything**
   - The draft avoids taking a stance anyone could challenge.

## Use

Do not treat one signal as proof. Look for clusters. The more signals stack together, the more the text reads as AI-assisted.
