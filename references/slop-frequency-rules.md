# Slop Frequency Rules

Use this file to detect repeated model habits, not isolated style choices.

A single em dash can be style. Five em dashes in a short post can be a model habit.
One contrast frame can work. Three contrast frames usually mean the structure is being outsourced to a template.

## What to flag

### 1. Repeated contrast frames

Flag when:
- `не X, а Y` or `не про X, а про Y` appears 2+ times
- `Это не X. Это Y.` appears more than once
- `Не X. Не Y. Просто Z.` appears more than once
- negative-triad constructions appear back to back

Do not flag a single earned contrast. Flag the pattern when it becomes the skeleton of the text.

### 2. Colon-led framing

Flag when:
- 3+ nearby paragraphs use `claim:` followed by explanation
- colons repeatedly appear after verbs or labels like `понятно:`, `вывод:`, `итог:`, `важно:`
- the draft starts to look like slide bullets instead of prose

If a list is genuinely useful, keep the list. If the colon only creates drama, flatten it into a normal sentence.

### 3. Pull-quote paragraph endings

Flag when:
- 2+ consecutive paragraphs end with a polished one-liner
- every section closes with a neat summary sentence
- the ending could be copied into a motivational carousel without context

A strong ending is fine. A row of quote-card endings usually feels manufactured.

### 4. Metronomic rhythm

Flag when:
- 4+ consecutive sentences are close in length and shape
- every paragraph has the same pace
- the draft reads smoothly but without pressure, pause, or friction

Fix rhythm by changing sentence structure, not by adding random slang or artificial roughness.

### 5. Repeated abstract nouns

Flag when the same abstract noun repeats across paragraphs without new concrete detail.

Watch words like:
- `развитие`
- `качество`
- `результат`
- `подход`
- `процесс`
- `система`
- `эффективность`
- `ценность`

Do not ban abstract nouns. Ask whether each use adds a new mechanism, example, number, scene, or constraint.

### 6. Rhetorical questions as scaffolding

Flag when:
- 2+ rhetorical questions are used as paragraph pivots in the same short text
- questions appear only to open the next block
- the answer could start directly without losing meaning

Do not flag questions in FAQ, lessons, tutorials, scripts, or genuine reader-objection handling.

### 7. Safe transitions repeated too often

Flag when connectors repeat without doing real logical work.

Watch phrases like:
- `таким образом`
- `следовательно`
- `важно отметить`
- `при этом`
- `в результате`
- `с другой стороны`

If the transition only announces logic, remove it or replace it with the actual link between ideas.

## Before replacing

Ask:
1. Is the repetition intentional and earned?
2. Does this match the writer's known samples, or does it look like a model habit?
3. Would removing one instance break the logic, or only reduce noise?
4. Is the repeated move carrying new meaning each time?

## Editing rule

Fix structure first. Wording second.

Do not flag isolated instances as frequency violations. One contrast frame, one em dash, or one rhetorical question is not the problem. The problem is when the same move becomes the hidden template of the text.
