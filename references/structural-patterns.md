# Structural AI Patterns

These are high-signal patterns that often survive simple word-level editing.

## Common patterns to remove

### 1. Contrast-for-depth pattern
- `Это не про X, это про Y`
- `Это не X. Это Y.`
- `X — это не про A, а про B`
- `It's not about X, it's about Y`

Use only when the contrast is genuinely earned. Most of the time, rewrite as a direct statement.

### 2. Negative triad pattern
- `Не X. Не Y. Просто Z.`
- `Not X. Not Y. Just Z.`

This often reads like recycled LinkedIn rhetoric.

### 3. Dramatic triple fragments
- `Фокус. Скорость. Результат.`
- `Clarity. Speed. Results.`

A three-beat fragment is not substance. Expand into an actual sentence unless the genre strongly supports punch fragments.

### 4. Rhetorical pause-answer
- `Результат? Рост конверсии.`
- `The result? Higher retention.`

Usually sounds performative. Rewrite as a normal sentence.

### 5. Repeated two-word echoes
- `Меньше шума. Меньше путаницы.`
- `More clarity. More control.`

Use sparingly. Repetition without pressure feels synthetic.

### 6. Faux-profound one-line paragraphs
- one short line
- another short line
- artificial emotional pause

If the pause carries no new meaning, collapse the lines.

### 7. Colon-led pseudo-structure inside short posts
- a strong claim followed by `:` in paragraph after paragraph
- mini-sections that sound like slide bullets rather than prose

In short posts, repeated colons often make the text feel outlined instead of written. Prefer normal sentence flow unless a list is genuinely needed.

### 8. Em-dash dependence
- `X — Y — Z`
- em dashes replacing commas in ordinary prose
- dramatic colon after verbs like `понимаешь:`, `видно:`, `ясно:` when a clause would read more naturally

If commas or sentence splits work, prefer them. Overused em dashes are a frequent AI smell in Russian drafts.

## Detection rule

When auditing, look beyond words. Ask:
- Is the sentence trying to sound sharp instead of saying something?
- Does the structure create fake drama?
- Would a direct sentence say the same thing better?
